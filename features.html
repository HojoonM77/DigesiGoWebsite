Core Feature

Conversational AI Logging

No forms. No tapping through dropdowns. Just tell DigestiGo what's happening in plain English — exactly how you'd describe it to a friend — and the AI categorises and saves it automatically.

  • Understands natural language and categorises entries as dietary, symptom, or trigger
  • A single message can span multiple categories — "I ate pizza and felt bloated" logs both dietary and trigger
  • Category badges appear on each AI response showing what was saved
  • Powered by Groq API using llama-3.3-70b for fast, accurate responses
  • Chat history persists across sessions so your log is always complete

Symptom Tracking

Structured symptom logging.

Just say how you feel in plain language — "I've been bloated all morning" — and DigestiGo logs it as a structured symptom record, building a timeline you can share with your doctor.

  • Logs common digestive symptoms: bloating, nausea, cramping, fatigue, reflux, and more
  • Symptoms are timestamped and listed in the Tracker dashboard for easy review

Trigger Detection

Spot what sets your symptoms off.

When a message implies a cause-and-effect relationship between food and a symptom — "pizza makes me bloated" — DigestiGo flags it as a trigger entry, categorised as both dietary and trigger simultaneously.

  • Multi-category entries: a single message can be dietary + trigger at the same time
  • Trigger summary format: "[Food] may trigger [symptom] — Take Caution!"
  • Triggers are stored separately and shown on the Tracker dashboard
  • Trigger list exported in the PDF report under Identified Trigger Factors

PDF Report Export

Share your health data with your doctor.

When you're ready, DigestiGo generates a formatted PDF report from everything in your log — structured for clinical appointments, with sections for symptoms, nutrition, triggers, and your own notes.

  • Section I: Clinical Symptoms — full timestamped symptom list
  • Section II: Nutritional Intake Analysis — carbs, proteins, dairy, fibre with counts and percentages
  • Section III: Identified Trigger Factors — all logged food-symptom cause-effect pairs
  • Section IV: Patient Notes — optional free-text field you write before exporting
  • One-tap share via iOS share sheet (email, AirDrop, print, etc.)

Under the Hood

How DigestiGo understands you.

DigestiGo is powered by the Groq API running llama-3.3-70b-versatile — a large language model that understands conversational health descriptions, classifies them into categories, and generates contextual, human-like responses in real time.

Read Our Story →

1. Message Received

Your plain-language message is sent to the Groq API along with a system prompt instructing the model to act as a digestive health assistant.

2. Category Classification

The model classifies the entry as dietary, symptom, trigger, or general — a single message can be assigned multiple categories simultaneously.

3. Sub-category Extraction

Dietary entries are further classified as carbs, proteins, dairy, or fibre. Trigger entries identify the cause food and the resulting symptom.

4. Structured Logging

Categorised entries are saved to persistent storage (AsyncStorage) so your log is preserved across sessions and available for the Tracker and Report screens.

5. Contextual Response

The model streams back a natural, health-focused reply confirming what was logged — with a relevant tip or follow-up observation based on the category detected.
